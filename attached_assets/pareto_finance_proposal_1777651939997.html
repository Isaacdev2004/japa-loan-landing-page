01 — Overview

Executive Summary

Pareto Finance Limited has a compelling product suite — 9 distinct loan products — and a clear mission to serve the underbanked in Nigeria. However, the current website falls significantly short of representing this ambition. It functions as a static brochure rather than an active business tool, failing to convert visitors into applicants and failing to communicate the trust signals a financial institution needs.

This proposal outlines the critical issues discovered during a deep technical and UX audit of every page, and recommends a phased set of improvements that will directly impact loan applications, customer confidence, and brand credibility.

Bottom line: The website has a functional foundation but needs significant structural, design, and functional upgrades to compete in today's digital-first financial services landscape and convert the traffic it receives into real business.

02 — Audit Findings

Critical Issues Discovered

After reviewing every page — Home, About, Board of Directors, Management Team, all 9 loan product pages, and Contact — the following issues were identified and classified by severity.

Critical

Broken Contact Page Title

The contact page title literally reads "Feel free to send ??? us a message" — with question marks visible in the browser tab. This is a trust-destroying error for a financial institution.

Critical

No Loan Application Form on Site

Most loan pages redirect to either the contact page or an external JotForm link. There is no native online application experience, causing drop-offs and loss of conversion data.

Critical

Inconsistent Phone Numbers

Two different phone numbers appear across the site — 080 265 43555 and 0703 251 3433 — with no explanation. This creates confusion and damages credibility.

Critical

URLs Contain Spaces

Page URLs like "board of directors.html" and "SME Loan.html" contain spaces, which break links in many environments, hurt SEO rankings, and look unprofessional.

Critical

No SSL / Security Trust Signals

A financial site asking users to share personal and financial information must display prominent security badges, regulatory licensing info, and data privacy assurances. None exist.

Moderate

Outdated Location/Branch Info

The About page states branch expansion plans "in 2022" — this is four years out of date, making the company look stagnant or neglected online.

Moderate

No Loan Calculator

Interest rates are mentioned (e.g., 4% monthly) but there is no interactive calculator. Competitors that offer this see dramatically higher engagement and applications.

Moderate

Thin Product Pages

Several loan pages (especially Esusu, Fast Track IOU) have almost no content — no eligibility criteria, required documents, or FAQs. This leaves visitors with unanswered questions.

Moderate

No SEO Optimization

Pages lack meta descriptions, structured data, and keyword optimization. The site is invisible to Nigerians searching for "quick business loans Lagos" or "payday loan Nigeria."

Moderate

No WhatsApp / Live Chat Integration

Nigerian customers heavily use WhatsApp. The absence of a WhatsApp button or live chat widget is a massive missed opportunity for lead capture.

Moderate

Google Maps Link is Generic

Both address links on the site point to maps.google.com with no specific location pinned. Customers cannot find the Ikeja or Ogun state offices.

Minor

No Social Media Links

The footer has no social media links despite this being a key trust and engagement channel for Nigerian financial brands.

Minor

Testimonials Lack Depth

Only 2 testimonials exist — one from "Andrew from Sagamu" and "Odun from XYZ Company." No photos (or consistent placeholders), surnames, or verified details.

Minor

Mobile Navigation Experience

The nested dropdown menus for 9 loan products are cumbersome on mobile, likely causing friction for the majority of Nigerian users who browse on phones.

03 — Recommendations

What I Propose to Build

Based on the audit, below are the concrete improvements I recommend. These are designed to maximize business impact — turning the website from a brochure into a loan-generating machine.

01

Full Website Redesign & Rebuild

A complete rebuild of all pages with a modern, trust-inspiring financial design. Clean, professional, mobile-first. Fix all URL structures, broken copy, and inconsistent information. Establish a consistent design system across all pages.

All Pages Mobile-First Design System URL Cleanup
02

Native Loan Application System

Replace the external JotForm redirect with a beautiful, multi-step native application form for each loan product. With smart conditional logic — applicants see only the fields relevant to their loan type. All submissions go directly to Pareto's dashboard or email, with automated confirmation messages to applicants.

Multi-Step Forms Email Notifications Per-Product Forms Lead Capture
03

Interactive Loan Calculator

An interactive calculator that lets prospects input their desired loan amount and tenure, then see the monthly repayment, total interest, and disbursement amount in real-time. This is the single highest-converting feature on any lending site — it turns curious visitors into committed applicants.

Real-Time Calculation Per-Product Rates CTA Integration
04

WhatsApp & Live Chat Integration

A sticky WhatsApp button linked to Pareto's business number, enabling one-tap conversations. Optionally, a lightweight chatbot that can answer FAQs (e.g., "What documents do I need for a payday loan?") and route qualified leads to the team. This can double inquiry volumes within weeks.

WhatsApp Business API FAQ Chatbot Lead Routing
05

SEO & Content Optimization

Comprehensive on-page SEO across all pages — meta titles, descriptions, structured data (schema markup for financial services), keyword-rich product page content, and a blog/news section for Pareto to publish financial tips, product updates, and success stories. This drives free, organic traffic from Google.

On-Page SEO Schema Markup Blog Section Google Indexing
06

Trust & Compliance Section

A dedicated section (and footer elements) displaying CBN licensing information, privacy policy, data protection (NDPR compliance), security certifications, and regulatory body links. In Nigeria's financial space, this is non-negotiable for customer confidence.

CBN License Display Privacy Policy NDPR Compliance Security Badges
07

Branch Locator & Accurate Maps

An interactive branch locator showing all offices — Head Office (Ikeja), Sagamu, Sango, Ota, and Ibafo — with real Google Maps pins, opening hours, and contact numbers specific to each branch. Customers can find their nearest office instantly.

Google Maps API Per-Branch Info Opening Hours
08

Customer Portal (Optional Phase 2)

A secure online portal where existing borrowers can check their loan balance, repayment schedule, and make online repayments. This dramatically reduces branch walk-ins for routine queries, saves staff time, and improves customer satisfaction — particularly for the younger, tech-savvy audience.

Secure Login Loan Dashboard Online Repayment Phase 2

07 — Why This Matters

A Note on Digital Trust in Nigerian Finance

Nigerian consumers are increasingly digital-savvy and financially sophisticated. They compare options online, read reviews, and make trust judgments within seconds of landing on a website. A microfinance institution with broken links, question marks in page titles, and no loan calculator will lose potential customers — not because the products are bad, but because the digital presentation doesn't inspire confidence.

Pareto Finance has already built the hard part: a strong founding team, a regulated business, 9 real products, 9,000+ satisfied customers, and a track record since 2019. The website should be working as hard as the team does. Right now, it isn't.

My commitment: To build a website that matches the quality and ambition of what Pareto Finance Limited has already built on the ground — one that makes every Nigerian who visits it feel confident enough to apply for a loan.
